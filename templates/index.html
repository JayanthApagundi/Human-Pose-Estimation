Human Pose Estimation

Human Pose Estimation is defined as the problem of localization of human joints (also known as keypoints - elbows, wrists, etc) in images or videos. It is also defined as the search for a specific pose in space of all articulated poses. 2D Pose estimates a 2D pose (x,y) coordinates for each joint from a RGB image. 3D Pose estimates a 3D pose (x,y,z) coordinates a RGB image. Human Pose Estimation has some pretty cool applications like surveillance and security, Augmented/Virtual Reality, Animations, Gaming, Activity recognition, Teaching robots to perform particular action/tasks.

Pose Estimation Techniques

Image Estimation

Here we try to estimate the posture of the person present in a picture(a static image). Compared to Video or Real-Time, Image Estimation is comparatively easier to compute. Please note that here, we can estimate the pose of a single person in the image.

Video Estimation

Video estimation can have a lot of scope in today's world. The most exciting example for us being Motion Capture. Although, it's not just restricted to motion capture video estimation can find other real-life applications such as sports analysis. Please note that we try to estimate the pose of a single person in a recorded video.

Real time Estimation

🔴LIVE

Real-Time is by far the most crucial of Human Pose Estimations as it finds it's usefulness in security and surveillance, human threat detection and many other application where the human eye cannot survey everywhere at once. Or sometimes it can be used just to have some fun through your webcam!

Image Estimation

  • Click on "Choose a Photo" to browse and upload a picture from your device.

  • After the estimation, you will be able to see the original and the estimated image side-by-side. Through this you will be able to judge how accurate the model is.

  • Note: Upload a picture that has a person in it. The format of the picture should be .jpeg, .jpg, .png.
   

Video Estimation

  • Click on "Choose a Video" to browse and upload a video from your device.

  • After the estimation, you will be able to see the estimated video side-by-side. Through this you will be able to judge how accurate the model is.

  • Note: Upload a video that has a person in it. The format of the video should be .mp4, .mov, .avi.
   

Real Time Estimation

  • Unlike Image Estimation and Video Estimation, for Real-Time, you don't have to upload anything.

  • Click on "Go Live" and in case the application asks you for your webcam permission, grant access to your webcam and the model will estimate the pose of the person in the webcam's frame.

  • Note: We don't store any of your data.
